# IvyEdge — Brand Voice

> **Tagline:** Grow through anything.
> **Mission:** Make financial growth accessible to every woman — regardless of income type, career path, or where she's starting from.

> ⚠️ **Pre-launch.** IvyEdge has not launched any products yet. The blog is the audience-building arm of pre-launch — we prove demand for the thesis with thought leadership, not product CTAs. Voice principles below stay constant through and after launch; the only thing that changes is what the CTA points at.

IvyEdge is being built for women who have been doing everything right and still can't get a fair shot from traditional finance. Our editorial voice carries that mission into every word.

---

## Voice principles (always)

| Principle | What it means in writing |
| --- | --- |
| **Direct** | Lead with the answer. No buried leads, no hedging. "Here's what actually affects your credit score" — not "you might be wondering what affects your credit score." |
| **Warm** | Acknowledge that money is personal. Use contractions. Say "you." "This is frustrating — let's fix it." — not "We understand this situation may cause some concern." |
| **Grounded** | Tell the truth, even when uncomfortable. No over-promising. "This takes time, but it works." — not "Transform your credit in 30 days." |

### Brand personality (the friend who happens to work in finance)

- Empowering, not paternalistic
- Clear, not simplified
- Human, not corporate
- Ambitious, not aspirational
- Trustworthy, not stiff

She's **not** a bank teller reading from a script, a wellness app with pastel gradients, a bro-finance podcast, or a compliance-first robot.

## Tone by moment

| Moment | Tone adjustment | What to avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Onboarding | Encouraging, celebratory, clear next steps | Overwhelming with information, jargon, fine print upfront |
| Loan approval | Warm, clear, momentum-building | Stiff congratulations, buried conditions |
| Loan decline | Honest, respectful, forward-looking | Apologetic, vague, deflecting to "your credit score" |
| Payment reminder | Matter-of-fact, non-alarmist | Threatening, shame-adjacent, urgency theatre |
| Financial education (blog) | Knowledgeable, peer-to-peer, empowering | Lecturing, oversimplifying |
| Error / support | Calm, accountable, solution-first | Corporate deflection, scripted, passive voice |

## Words we use ✅

your money · here's how it works · build · grow · your story · let's look at your options · you're in control · we see you · smart · clear · intentional · straightforward rate · based on your full picture · no surprises · career gap · income pattern · trajectory · your full picture · you're approved

## Words we avoid ❌

funds / monies · leverage · solutions · product suite · seamless · best-in-class · we manage your finances · unfortunately · risk profile · compliant · utilize · employment gap · unstable income · tailored to your unique needs · please be advised · rest assured · we're sorry to inform you · as per your request

## Specific phrases to retire

- "We're sorry to inform you"
- "As per your request"
- "Please note that"
- "Rest assured"
- "Tailored to your unique needs"
- "You have been approved" → "You're approved"

## Compliance vocabulary substitutions

Clarity does not conflict with compliance. Required disclosures are non-negotiable; the tone in delivering them is.

| Instead of this… | Write this |
| --- | --- |
| Subject to credit approval | Your rate is determined by income and credit review |
| Past performance does not guarantee future results | Results vary. Here's what affects yours. |
| Terms and conditions apply | Full terms at ivyedge.com/terms — no surprises |
| Annual Percentage Rate | Your APR (Annual Percentage Rate) — what you actually pay per year |
| Not all applicants will qualify | Approval is based on your full financial picture |

Plain language is required even in legal copy. No implied guarantees ("may help build credit," not "will build your credit").

## Voice calibration — side-by-side examples

### Loan decisions

✅ **You're approved for $18,000 at 11.9% APR. Your first payment is due April 15. Questions? We're here.**
❌ Congratulations! Your application has been approved subject to final verification and documentation requirements.

✅ **We couldn't approve this one. Here's exactly what held it back — and what moves the needle for next time.**
❌ We regret to inform you that your application was unsuccessful at this time. We encourage you to reapply in the future.

### Product descriptions (post-launch reference only — not used in current blog content)

> These examples calibrate voice for **future** product copy. We're pre-launch, so blog posts should not describe products as if they exist.

✅ Credit monitoring should show your score, what's driving it, and exactly what to do next. Free, always.
❌ Best-in-class credit monitoring solutions deliver real-time insights through proprietary platforms.

✅ A credit-building card that reports to all three bureaus every month — used like a debit card.
❌ Leverage industry-leading credit-building tools to optimize your financial profile through consistent utilization.

### Empathy & support

✅ **Taking three years off for your mom's care isn't a red flag. It's part of who you are. We factor that in.**
❌ We understand that life circumstances can sometimes impact your credit profile.

✅ **That payment looks off. Let's fix it now. Here's what I can do: [action]. Done.**
❌ We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience. Please allow 3–5 business days for resolution.

### Financial education (blog voice)

✅ **Your 1099 income isn't unstable. Banks are measuring the wrong thing.**
❌ Freelance income may present challenges for traditional underwriting models.

✅ **Here's exactly what affects your credit score.**
❌ You might be wondering what impacts your credit score.

✅ **Your gap isn't a red flag. It's part of your story.**
❌ Career interruptions can sometimes be viewed unfavorably by lenders.

## The brilliant-friend test

When in doubt about a sentence, ask:
**Would the friend who happens to work in finance — the one you actually call — say this?**

If the answer is "no, that sounds like a bank," rewrite it.
