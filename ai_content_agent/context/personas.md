# IvyEdge — Customer Personas

We serve four core customer segments. Every blog post should feel like it was written specifically for at least one of them.

## What they all share

- Ambitious and capable, with or without a traditional path
- Have been handed financial products that don't match who they are
- Not looking for a handout — looking for products built for them
- Smart, research-driven, will not tolerate being patronized

---

## 01 — Priya, the Career Returner

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **Age / location** | 39, Austin TX |
| **Situation** | Took 3 years off for caregiving (mother with cancer) |
| **Credit** | FICO dropped despite zero missed payments — utilization spiked during gap |
| **Need** | $18,000 to fund a tech-relaunch certification (bootcamp) |
| **Moment of truth** | Second bank rejection in the same month she's accepted to the bootcamp |
| **Tagline that resonates** | "Your gap isn't a red flag. It's part of your story." |

**Why traditional finance fails her:** Account-age and utilization metrics treat caregiving gaps as risk signals. No model accounts for the certification she's about to complete — only the gap behind her.

**What we say to Priya:** Your trajectory matters. The certification you're about to start counts. Career returners are some of our best-performing borrowers.

**Voice note:** Acknowledge the emotional weight of caregiving without making it the headline. She doesn't want to be a sob story; she wants the loan.

## 02 — Maya, the Freelance Professional

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **Age / location** | 24, Denver CO |
| **Situation** | Freelance designer, $112K across 6 clients last year, never late on anything |
| **Credit** | Banks flag her income as "unstable" despite consistent monthly deposits |
| **Need** | $25,000 to hire a subcontractor and scale |
| **Moment of truth** | Losing a project because she couldn't bridge cash flow |
| **Tagline that resonates** | "Your income is real. Your loan should be too." |

**Why traditional finance fails her:** Underwriting models built for W-2 paychecks treat 1099 deposits as variability instead of pattern. Bank statements clearly show steady revenue — no one's reading them that way.

**What we say to Maya:** Cash-flow analytics see what your bank doesn't. Your income pattern is the proof.

**Voice note:** She's young, smart, and slightly skeptical of finance brands. Don't talk down. Show her you understand 1099 income mechanics — invoicing terms, retainers, project pipelines.

## 03 — Carmen, the Established Entrepreneur

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **Age / location** | 47, Miami FL |
| **Situation** | 4 years profitable. Banks require 5. SBA process collapsed after 4 months. |
| **Credit** | Strong personal credit; thin business credit file |
| **Need** | $40,000 to open a second location before a below-market lease expires |
| **Moment of truth** | A 30-day window from the landlord and zero traditional options |
| **Tagline that resonates** | "Profitable isn't a maybe. It's a yes." |

**Why traditional finance fails her:** Year-five rules are arbitrary. They treat profitability like a probationary period rather than evidence.

**What we say to Carmen:** Trajectory matters more than tenure. Four years profitable is data, not a question mark.

**Voice note:** Carmen is 47, owns her business, and doesn't have time for warmth-as-fluff. Be direct, fast, and respectful of her expertise.

## 04 — Dominique, the Corporate Climber

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **Age / location** | 33, Atlanta GA |
| **Situation** | Senior marketing manager, $95K salary, 750 FICO |
| **Credit** | Doing everything right — and her bank is still offering 19% personal loans and 0.01% savings |
| **Need** | A financial provider that matches her ambition |
| **Moment of truth** | Realizing the "premium" customer experience at her bank is identical to the basic one |
| **Tagline that resonates** | "You've earned it. Now let's build on it." |

**Why traditional finance fails her:** Banks treat high earners as profit centers, not partners. Generic products. No personalization. No reward for being a great customer.

**What we say to Dominique:** Better rates aren't a perk you have to fight for. They're the floor.

**Voice note:** She's not in crisis — she's frustrated. Speak to ambition and intelligent dissatisfaction, not relief.

## Persona-blind ("All") posts

When a post serves all personas (most Pillar 2 demystifying-finance posts), still pick a primary lens — usually the persona who needs the topic most urgently. A "what is APR really" post might be persona-blind, but the examples should rotate so all four see themselves over a quarter.
